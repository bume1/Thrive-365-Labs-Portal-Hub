Timeline: 10-Phase Implementation Process Flow

Phase 1: Contract & Initial Setup
  • Sales closes deal and executes contract
  • Senior Leadership approves contract terms
  • Accounting processes initial contract financing
  • Sales provides pre-install checklist, client profile, ancillary supply list
  • Client PM completes client profile and pre-installation form
  • Implementations verifies pre-install checklist, assesses CLIA needs, plans integrations
  • Implementations defines internal launch team roles
  • BAA signed
  • HubSpot/client profile setup completed
Phase 2: Financials, CLIA & Hiring
  • Implementations submits CLIA upgrade paperwork (CMS-116)
  • Client PM connects with billing support team
  • Accounting establishes invoice contact, CPT verifications, fee schedule
  • Client PM coordinates Lab Tech hiring with Implementations support
  • Client PM confirms Lab Director participation, obtains CV
  • Client PM submits current lab test menu for CMS-116
  • Senior Leadership processes CLIA service invoicing
Phase 3: Welcome Call
  • Implementations hosts Welcome Call
  • Build rapport with all stakeholders
  • Provide 30,000 ft overview of launch process (Welcome Call deck)
  • Set expectations for next steps
  • All stakeholders attend: Sales, Senior Leadership, Accounting, Implementations, Customer Success, Client (PM + Lab Tech + IT Contact), Clinical Specialists, Field Service Engineer, Lab Director, Instrument Engineers
Phase 4: Tech Infrastructure & LIS Integration + Inventory Forecasting (Weekly Implementation Calls)
  • Weekly Implementation Calls (~1 hour on mutual day):
  • Implementations leads collaborative logistics planning
  • Topics: LIS/EHR/Instrument Integration, CLIA Upgrade & Lab Setup, Pre-Installation Assessments, Materials Prep, Installation Prep
  • Implementations conducts LIS/EHR integration planning, interface meetings, integration syncs
  • IT Contact coordinates network/system requirements with Implementations
  • Instrument Engineers align on technical requirements
  • Senior Leadership handles vendor contract negotiation for instrument and materials
  • Senior Leadership orders analyzer
  • Senior Leadership confirms monthly/yearly test volume projections
  • Implementations completes virtual lab space assessment
  • Implementations prepares client portal inventory tracking
  • Clinical Application Specialists define validation kit needs
  • Sales defines live testing kit needs based on projections
  • Accounting processes ongoing financing and vendor payments
  • Customer Success joins calls to stay aware of support targets and upsell opportunities
  • Sales may optionally attend for learning/awareness and upsell opportunities
Ongoing collaborative calls between Welcome Call and Onboarding Call
Phase 5: Onboarding Call
  • Implementations hosts Onboarding Call
  • Present detailed launch plan with explicit timeline (Onboarding Call deck)
  • Key dates confirmed: delivery → installation → validation → go-live → 90-day optimization
  • Debut final project plan
  • All stakeholders attend: Sales, Senior Leadership, Accounting, Implementations, Customer Success, Client (PM + Lab Tech + IT Contact), Clinical Specialists, Field Service Engineer, Lab Director, Instrument Engineers
Phase 6: Supply Orders & Logistics
  • Senior Leadership orders all analyte materials (validation kits, sample kits, live testing supplies)
  • Accounting reviews and sends formal POs
  • Implementations confirms installation dates with FSE and client
  • Implementations confirms CAS availability for validation support
  • Client PM confirms ancillary supplies: DI water source, table placement
  • Clinical Application Specialists track validation and sample kit delivery
  • Client-facing and internal KPIs solidified
Phase 7: Virtual Soft Pilot & Delivery
  • Field Service Engineer delivers analyzer to site
  • Accounting processes delivery payment
  • Field Service Engineer executes physical installation of instrument
  • IT Contact ensures network connectivity
  • Implementations coordinates AnyDesk remote access setup
  • IT Contact performs LIS connection compatibility check
  • Clinical Application Specialists confirm validation kit receipt
  • Clinical Application Specialists provide documentation: material inserts, COAs, SOP templates
  • Client Lab Tech confirms urine sample source (30-50 minimum)
  • Ancillary supplies delivered and verified
  • Pre-launch feedback and Q&A session
Phase 8: Training & Full Validation
  • Field Service Engineer configures systems and parameters, sets up reagents/cals/QCs
  • Clinical Application Specialists provide technical training to Lab Tech
  • Client Lab Tech PERFORMS all validation tests with CAS support:
  • Intraprecision and Interprecision studies
  • Linearity, Specificity, Interference Mix
  • Limit of Detection (LOD) and Limit of Blank (LOB)
  • Patient correlation studies
  • Implementations trains Lab Tech on client portal: inventory management, reporting, customer success features
  • Implementations oversees LIS/EHR/EMR integration with IT Contact and Instrument Engineers
  • IT Contact oversees integration between network, instrument, and LIS/EHR
  • Instrument Engineers provide EHR/LIS vendor support and API configuration
  • Customer Success supports LIS integration as resource
  • Clinical Application Specialists create validation package
  • Lab Director reviews and signs validation package (CRITICAL GATE)
Validation + Training must be complete and signed before go-live authorization
Phase 9: Go-Live
  • Lab Director authorizes go-live (validation complete + training complete)
  • Implementations and Customer Success co-oversee go-live execution
  • IT Contact provides go-live IT support
  • Go-live day support from Implementations and Customer Success
  • Client Lab Tech processes first live patient samples
  • Client begins live operations
Phase 10: Post-Launch Support & Optimization (90-Day Period)
  • Customer Success leads Week 1 and Week 2 check-in calls
  • Implementations and Customer Success collaborate on biweekly check-ins
  • Customer Success conducts 30-day performance review with formal feedback
  • Customer Success conducts 60-day performance review with formal feedback
  • Customer Success conducts 90-day final review with formal feedback
  • Customer Success manages post-launch monitoring and account optimization
  • Sales serves as Account Executive for relationship management
  • IT Contact provides ongoing system connectivity support
  • Instrument Engineers available for technical troubleshooting
  • Clinical Application Specialists provide ongoing technical support
  • Accounting manages post-launch billing
  • Customer Success handoff complete - full account management transition

Gantt Chart Key
Diamond Milestone: Key decision points (Welcome Call, Onboarding Call, Delivery, Go-Live)
Colored Bars: Phase duration and timeline
Overlapping Phases: Many activities run in parallel (e.g., CLIA hiring during tech infrastructure planning)
Timeline Notes:
  • Welcome Call: Scheduled for Week 2 after initial contract setup
  • Onboarding Call (*Week 5-6): Scheduled only once instrument delivery date is confirmed (typically Week 5-6)
  • Phase 4 (Weekly Implementation Calls): Runs continuously from Welcome Call through Onboarding Call
  • Phase 6 (Supply Orders): Overlaps with Phase 4 as materials are ordered during weekly planning
  • Phase 7 (Virtual Soft Pilot): Weeks 4-5 - Analyzer delivered, installed, and remote pilot completed BEFORE CAS arrives onsite
  • Phase 8 (Validation & Training): Weeks 6-8 - CAS provides 3-5 days of hands-on training within the full 7-14 day validation window. Lab Tech performs all validation tests with CAS support during this period.
  • Phase 9 (Go-Live): Week 8 - Occurs after validation is complete and signed by Lab Director
  • Phase 10: Extends beyond Week 8 for full 90-day post-launch optimization period
  • Typical Timeline: 30-60 days from contract to go-live (8 weeks average), with variations based on CLIA processing, hiring, and vendor lead times
